Championing the Vulnerable – A Career in Public Service to the Indigent

For over three decades, Phyllis Joan Powers, Esquire, LLC., has dedicated her legal career to serving those who are often overlooked in our society. With a profound commitment to justice, she has represented indigent clients in felony and misdemeanor cases, family law matters, and immigration petitions, tirelessly advocating for individuals who might otherwise go unheard.

Beyond she courtroom, Phyllis Joan Powers, Esquire, LLC., has devoted significant time to volunteer work, particularly with Philadelphia’s most vulnerable communities. From assisting the elderly in assisted living facilities to providing free legal services for victims of domestic abuse, children, and youth, her volunteer work has had a lasting impact. Notably, her contributions has ensured and provided free legal services to those affected by elder abuse, and other systemic challenges for over three decades.

Her community involvement extends into education, mentoring students and encouraging independence through legal literacy and life skills programs. By bridging legal expertise with compassion, Phyllis Joan Powers has not only advocated for justice but has also empowered individuals and families to navigate complex legal systems with confidence.

Her lifelong and career dedication exemplifies how one legal professional can transform communities, proving that the law is not just about rules but about people. Phyllis Powers’ career is a testament to the power of public service and the profound difference it can make in the lives of those who need it most.
